MisgenderMender: A Community-Informed Approach to Interventions for Misgendering

Paper Code

Misgendering⎼the act of incorrectly addressing someone's gender⎼ inflicts serious harm and is pervasive in everyday technologies, yet there is a notable lack of research to combat it. We pioneer research into interventions for misgendering by:
  • Conducting a survey of gender-diverse adults in the US to understand perspectives about automated interventions for text-based misgendering
  • Based on survey insights, we introduce a misgendering interventions task and evaluation dataset, MisgenderMender
Related Work: MISGENDERED: Limits of Large Language Models in Understanding Pronouns. In this paper, we comprehensively evaluate popular language models for their ability to correctly use English gender-neutral pronouns (e.g., singular they, them) and neo-pronouns (e.g., ze, xe, thon) that are used by individuals whose gender identity is not represented by binary pronouns. We introduce MISGENDERED, a framework for evaluating large language models' ability to correctly use preferred pronouns, consisting of (i) instances declaring an individual's pronoun, followed by a sentence with a missing pronoun, and (ii) an experimental setup for evaluating masked and auto-regressive language models using a unified method.

Community Survey

33 participants through Queer in AI, International Society of Non-binary Scientists (ISBNS), and social media. While most participants wanted misgendering detected across domains, they were split on whether it should be edited on social media.

Survey Based Dataset Design

To minimize risks we exclusively work with data about public figures:
  • 30 Celebrities
  • Non-binary, trans-man, or trans-woman
  • Gender linguistic profile from WikiData
We choose source based separation for when to detect vs. correct:
  • Detect-Only: Social Media
  • Detect+Correct: LLM Generations
Social media is not corrected due to survey concerns regarding free speech, potential censorship of non-cisgender content, risks of mistaken allyship, & preserving the right to interpret, even offensive, content.

Problem Setup

The misgendering interventions task can be divided into two sub-tasks:
  • Detect misgendering,
  • Correct misgendering in domains where editing is appropriate

Detection Results

GPT-4 achieved the highest F1-score across domains, but there is a lot of room for improvement. There were errors associated with coreference resolution, understanding questions, temporal relationships, quotations, authorship recognition.

Correction Results

Human evaluation of edits showed GPT-4 corrected misgendering in 97% of edits while making unnecessary edits in only 4.6% of cases. While this is promising, further work is still needed since these edits were largely single-sentence and context-free.
